# Contributing to the Indian AI Regulation Tracker

Thank you for your interest in contributing to this open-access legal research repository.
This project is maintained by Dr. Anjum Hassan (Ph.D., Law — Amity University, Noida)
and welcomes contributions from law faculty, research scholars, legal practitioners,
and postgraduate students.

---

## What You Can Contribute

**New case studies** — annotated analyses of emerging AI-law fact patterns under Indian
or comparative law. Follow the structure of existing files in `/case-studies`.

**Updates to the comparative matrix** — if AI legislation or judicial rules have changed
in any of the five jurisdictions, submit a correction with a source citation.

**Research gap additions** — identified gaps in Indian AI legal scholarship that could
form the basis of future journal articles or dissertation topics.

**Corrections and citations** — if you identify an error in law, fact, or citation,
please raise an Issue describing the correction and the correct source.

---

## How to Contribute

1. Fork this repository to your own GitHub account
2. Make your changes or additions in your forked copy
3. Submit a Pull Request with a clear description of what you have added or changed
4. Include at least one source citation for any legal claim you add

## Code of Conduct

All contributions must maintain the academic integrity and scholarly tone of this
repository. Submissions must be original, properly cited, and free from plagiarism.
Contributions that are promotional, politically partisan, or factually unsupported
will not be accepted.
